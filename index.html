I'm an AI engineer and founder working at the frontier of autonomous systems. My work spans the full stack — from enterprise automation platforms to programming language design — unified by one conviction: production AI should be treated as applied physics, not fashion.

At Prescott Data, I lead as Founder and Lead AI Engineer, building Dromos — a platform that automates complex end-to-end workflows and turns unstructured data into structured insight at enterprise scale.

With AI Dojo, I publish research papers and run masterclasses on the real physics of AI engineering: attention, token limits, drift, and agent architecture — for engineers who want to understand, not just ship.

And with Turn, I designed a systems programming language specifically for agentic computation — Erlang-style actors, stochastic inference, and durable memory as first-class bytecode primitives. Built on Rust. No frameworks required.
